Rani Lakshmi Bai

Why I Admire Her

Rani Lakshmi Bai, born as Manikarnika in 1828, was the fearless queen of Jhansi who led her army against British forces during the Indian Rebellion of 1857. She became an icon of resistance and an inspiration to millions.

What I deeply admire about her is her courage, leadership, and love for her motherland. At a young age, she took responsibility not only as a queen but also as a military leader. Even with a child tied on her back, she charged into battle.

Her famous words — “I shall not surrender my Jhansi” — still echo in history as a symbol of unbreakable will and patriotism. She sacrificed her life but left behind a legacy of inspiration that continues to empower women and youth today.

A humble tribute to Rani Lakshmi Bai — a legend of India's freedom struggle.
